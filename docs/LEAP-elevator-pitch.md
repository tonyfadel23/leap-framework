# LEAP Elevator Pitch
## Three versions: 15 seconds, 60 seconds, 3 minutes

---

## The 15-Second Version

LEAP is an AI-first product discovery system that replaces the roadmap with a learning loop — where AI executes the research, prototyping, and evidence gathering, and the PM's only job is to decide: ship, iterate, pivot, or kill.

---

## The 60-Second Version

**The problem:** Product teams ship features without evidence. The roadmap is a graveyard of unvalidated assumptions — and every AI tool on the market just helps you fill that graveyard faster.

**The framework:** LEAP — Learn the problem, Explore the options, Action the plan, Prove it. Three stages and one continuous evaluation layer. AI runs the execution: parallel analysis, prototype generation, quality scoring. The PM stops writing documents and starts making decisions.

**The insight:** This is not AI bolted onto the existing PM workflow. LEAP was designed from scratch assuming AI exists — the way an automobile was designed around the engine, not by strapping a motor onto a horse carriage. Remove the AI and you don't get a simpler framework. You get nothing.

**The metric that matters:** Bets resolved per quarter. Not features shipped. A team resolving 15 bets with evidence will outperform a team shipping 50 features without signal. Every single time.

## The 3-Minute Version

### The story that explains everything

A team pitched me a recommendation engine. Beautiful. Personalized. AI-powered. They had the tech spec, the competitive analysis, the revenue projection. I asked one question: "What relationship stage are we in with this customer segment?"

The answer was Functional. Customers couldn't find their last order.

The Customer Relationship Hierarchy — Functional, Trustworthy, Engaging, Valuable, Loved — killed that project in one question. You don't build personalization when search is broken. You don't build delight when customers can't trust your delivery time. That team was about to spend three months building a feature nobody needed yet. One framework, one question, zero wasted quarters.

### The diagnosis

That story is not an exception. It is the default mode of product teams everywhere. Teams ship features without evidence. They skip the problem and jump to the solution. Every AI tool on the market accelerates this dysfunction — write a PRD faster, summarize interviews quicker, auto-generate acceptance criteria. AI as autocomplete for the same broken process.

The roadmap is not the answer. It is the problem.

### The framework

LEAP replaces the roadmap with a learning loop. Four words. Three stages. One evaluation layer.

**Learn** the problem — score opportunities by Total Addressable Pain, not Total Addressable Market. Pain drives behavior change. Revenue projections don't. Filter by the Customer Relationship Hierarchy before spending a single hour on solutions.

**Explore** the options — generate at least three materially different solutions. Pre-mortem each one. Classify the risks: Tigers, Paper Tigers, Elephants. Pick the approach with the best risk-to-signal ratio.

**Action** the plan — every solution becomes a bet with a size that matches your confidence. Scout (hours), Sprint (weeks), Campaign (month). Define the hypothesis. Define the kill criteria. Before you start — not after sunk cost bias has set in.

**Prove** it — not a final step. A continuous evaluation layer across all three stages. At every point: should we continue, change direction, or stop? When a bet resolves, the decision routes: ship, iterate, pivot, or kill. The kill is the most valuable outcome. A kill with evidence saves months of building the wrong thing.

### The AI-first architecture

Here is where LEAP diverges from everything else on the market. This is not a PM framework with AI features added. It was designed from the ground up assuming AI exists.

The analogy: you can strap a motor onto a horse carriage. That is augmentation. Or you can design a vehicle from scratch that assumes the engine is there — four wheels, a transmission, a chassis built for speed. That is an automobile. Every AI product tool today is a motorized carriage. LEAP is the automobile.

Three AI agents analyze the same opportunity in parallel — market lens, user lens, systems lens — then a synthesis agent merges their perspectives. Interactive prototypes are generated in hours, not weeks. Quality gates score every artifact before it publishes. The PM does not write. The PM decides.

### The compound effect

After three quarters, the flywheel is spinning. Every resolved bet adds evidence to the tree. Every kill adds a rationale. The AI reads all of it before generating anything new. The tenth bet is smarter than the first because it stands on nine previous cycles.

More resolved bets. More evidence. Better-informed future bets. Faster resolution. More resolved bets. That is a compounding loop — and it is the structural advantage no feature factory can replicate.

### The metric

Bets resolved per quarter. Not features shipped. Not story points. Not velocity. A team resolving 15 bets with evidence will outperform a team shipping 50 features without signal. Every single time. Because the team with evidence knows what to build next. The team without evidence is guessing — faster.

### The close

Twenty years of product experience designed the decision architecture. AI designed the execution architecture. LEAP is what happens when both are built at the same time, for the same system, from the ground up.

LEAP: Learn the problem. Explore the options. Action the plan. Prove it — always.
